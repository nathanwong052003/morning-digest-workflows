# News Quality Guide

Use this reference when selecting and summarizing news for the morning digest.

## Source hierarchy

| Priority | Source type | Use case |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Primary sources such as official agencies, regulators, courts, company filings, official blogs, and speeches | Use for policy decisions, rules, enforcement actions, releases, and direct announcements. |
| 2 | Established newsrooms with named reporters and editorial standards | Use for verified reporting, context, and developments not yet documented in primary materials. |
| 3 | Specialist trade publications and research organizations | Use for technology, industry, and policy analysis when they have clear sourcing. |
| 4 | Social posts, anonymous commentary, reposts, or aggregators | Use only as leads; verify through stronger sources before including. |

## Inclusion criteria

Include a news item only when it meets most of these criteria:

| Criterion | Standard |
| --- | --- |
| Relevance | The item materially affects technology, politics, policy, markets, governance, security, privacy, AI, platforms, or public institutions. |
| Timeliness | Prefer items published or updated within the past 24 hours for a daily digest. Older items may be included when they remain newly relevant. |
| Verification | Major claims are confirmed by a primary source or at least two credible independent reports. |
| Practical value | The item helps the user understand what changed, why it matters, or what to watch next. |
| Non-duplication | Avoid repeating the same story from multiple outlets unless differing details or perspectives are important. |

## Summarization standard

For each included item, write a compact summary that answers four questions: what happened, who is affected, why it matters, and what may happen next. Identify uncertainty clearly. Use source links in the digest so the user can inspect the original reporting.

## Red flags

Avoid or downgrade items with sensational framing, anonymous single-source claims, unclear publication date, missing author or organization, obvious copied content, no link to primary materials for legal or policy claims, or major factual disagreement that cannot be resolved before publication.

## Suggested source mix

For technology, combine at least one broad technology news source with primary company, standards, regulatory, or security-advisory sources when available. For politics and policy, prioritize official government, legislative, regulatory, court, and election sources, then add reputable reporting for context.
